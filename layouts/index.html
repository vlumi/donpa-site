Three board families

Basic presets, square Grid, and hexagonal Hive — each with a Flat or Round edge. A Round board wraps: pan off one side and it flows in from the other, forever.

From a tap to a million cells

Sizes run XS to XXXL. The big boards pan and zoom with a corner minimap you can drag to navigate — and stay smooth on a phone. Every board keeps its own game going, so a quick round never costs you the huge one you've been chipping at for weeks.

Your Service Record

Best times, clears, full career stats — and pace, your required-moves-per-second rate that board luck can't inflate, recent and best per board. Filter by family, expand any board for its own history. It's your data, kept on your device (and your iCloud, if you sync) — never sent to us.

Friendly rivalry

Swap score cards with a player in the same room — no server, no accounts. Whoever gets your card becomes a rival: compare head-to-head and see where you rank on every board. Their scores stay theirs, yours stay yours.

One board a day

The daily challenge deals everyone the same board — same layout, same luck. Study it freely before you press Start (the clock measures pure execution), retry as often as you like, and keep the day's best time and pace. A calendar reaches back to day one, showing up daily builds your streak, and your rivals' dailies join the head-to-head.

Luck, tracked honestly

Sometimes no safe move exists and you have to guess. Donpa computes the exact odds your click had at that moment and keeps score — guesses faced, survived, and your luckiest escape. Survive a long shot and the game says so on the spot; six difficulties climb all the way to Lunatic, where the board fights back.

iPhone, iPad & Mac

A native app on every screen — touch on iPhone and iPad, mouse or full keyboard play on Mac (a hardware keyboard works on iPad too), and the board plays with VoiceOver, cell by cell. Free on all of them, with your records syncing across your devices through your own iCloud.

Free. No ads, no accounts, no tracking. Just mines.
